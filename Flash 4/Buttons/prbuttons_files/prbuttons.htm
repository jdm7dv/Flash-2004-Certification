Animated buttons really aren't that tricky! Just keep your head cold, and you'll be animating in minutes.

Step 1. Create a button by pressing F8.

Step 2. You should now see 4 keyframes. Up, Over, Down, Hit.

  • Up is the standard look of the button.
  • Over is when the mouse is over.
  • Down is when mouse button is clicked over.
  • Hit, is where the button is "activated" - move the mouse over this area to activate the over area.

Step 3. Let's try animating the "Over" state. Press F8 and create a Movie Clip.

Step 4. Make the animation you wish to have on the Over state. NOTE: This animation, should be made completely seperate of the Button... They will be combined later.

When created, you should have TWO files in you library:

Step 5. Tricky part: Go to where you edit your button. Drag your recently created button animation to the over state:

Step 6. Now you have an animated button. Let's test it. Press CTRL + ENTER, and test it.

NOTE: Animated buttons WILL NOT WORK, with only the "buttons enabled" checked. You have to export it.

This is important - it's the premier reason people don't know how to animate their buttons. You HAVE TO EXPORT!

Voila!

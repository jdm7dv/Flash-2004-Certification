Tracing 3D

If your 3D program does not have the ability to export as a vectorbased image, or if you for any other reason would like to make 3D out of a bitmap, this is how you do it.

There are three different ways to tracing a bitmap:

Handtracing:

Haven't done much of it, but supposedly, you import your 3D image in Flash, and create a new layer, and by hand using Flash's drawing tools, draw the outlines of the 3D image, and paint it afterwards. Effective and timeconsuming.

Tracing using Flash:

Flash has a built in trace bitmap function. It allows you to trace a bitmap image, thereby making it vectorbased. This is also timeconsuming, as you have to optimize your vector afterwards, by straightening lines, and deleting unnecessary parts.

Tracing using a 3rd party program:

You can also trace your 3d bitmaps using another program than Flash, e.g. Streamline. Simply use Streamline to trace your bitmaps, and export them as a sequence of .ai files (vector based file). Then import in Flash, and voila!

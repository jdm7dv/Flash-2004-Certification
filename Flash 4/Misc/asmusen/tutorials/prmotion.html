Motion tweening

printable version

Downloadable FLA

Tweening motion is one of the forces of Flash. Simple but effective. What it does is, that you create a start frame and an ending frame, and Flash makes the animation!

Step 1. Create a symbol by pressing F8. Name it "motion".

Step 2. Make a start keyframe, by pressing F6 at the desired location.

Step 3. Drag your motion symbol into this frame from the library.

Step 4. Make an ending keyframe, by pressing F6 where you want the animation to end. This will duplicate the symbol from the first keyframe you made.

Step 5. Make your desired transformations to the symbol, which you want Flash to calculate. The allowed changes are: Scale, Rotate and Skew.

Step 6. Click properties for the empty frames between the first and the last keyframes. Select the tab "Tweening". Select "Motion Tween". If you want the symbol to rotate, choose between clockwise and counterclockwise and enter amount of times. Click OK.

That's it. Now you've made your animation.

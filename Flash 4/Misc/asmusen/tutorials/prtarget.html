Using Tell Target

printable version

Downloadable FLA

Tell target is probably the most advanced and useful feature in Flash. Also one of the most tricky to learn though... What it does, is that it sends information to the timeline inside a movieclip. This enables you to e.g. create a movie clip, which you can start and stop using seperate buttons.

This tutorial will help you create a music loop which you can stop from your main movie.

Step 1. Create a movie clip containing the music loop (labelled "music"), and a blank frame (labelled "blank").

Step 2. From the library, drag this symbol into your main movie, and place it.

Step 3. Click properties for this movie clip, and in the definition tab, fill the "Instance name&qUnt; dialog with "music".

Step 4. Make an on and an off for the music, and place them where you wan't them.

Step 5. Click properties for the on button, and give it the action "Tell Target":

On(Release)
Begin Tell Target [/music]
Goto and Play [music]
End Tell Target
End on

Now your on button will tell the music movie clip, to go and play where you labelled it "music". This will start the music.

Step 6. Repeat the process for the off button:

On(Release)
Begin Tell Target [/music]
Goto and Stop [blank]
End Tell Target
End on

This will make your off button tell your music movie clip to go and stop where you labelled a blank frame "blank". So it stops the music.

Voila. That's it.

JavaScript Math Object

Are you ready to begin again? Just when we thought it was safe to swim the warm waters, from out of the depths lurks ... the Java-DOM-Scripter, whose main function is to entwine with your present programming skills to squeeze the lifeblood from bulky ActionScripts!

When this encapsulation is complete, our DOM thief stores it's booty with the other found trophies in it's treasure chest, making each condensed function available to become the object of focus at any given moment. This is the essence of object-oriented programming, which is Macromedia's new vision for future scripting in the Flash 5 environment.

Expressions are interpreted by JavaScript with the Math object. Often-used properties are written in upper case to flag them as constants for the interpreter to parse before continuing to the other methods and scripted functions. The following properties and methods are available as functions for programming in the JavaScript environment:

Properties - constants:

E - Euler's constant
PI - pi
LN10 - the natural logarithm of 10
LN2 - the natural logarithm of 2
LOG10E - the base 10 logarithm of e
LOG2E - the base 2 logarithm of e
SQRT1_2 - the square root of 1/2
SQRT2 - the square root of 2

Trigonometry - angles measured in radians:

sin(x) - sine of x
cos(x) - cosine of x
tan(x) - tangent of x
asin(x) - inverse sine of x
acos(x) - inverse cosine of x
atan(x) - inverse tangent of x, where -pi/2 <= x <= pi/2
atan2(x) - inverse tangent of x, where -pi <= x <= pi

Exponentiation/logarithms:

log(x) - natural logarithm of x
exp(x) - Euler's constant to the x power
pow(x,y) - x to the y power
sqrt(x) - the square root of x

Miscellaneous:

abs(x) - absolute value of x
max(x,y) - larger of two numbers
min(x,y) - smaller of two numbers
ceil(x) - the smallest integer greater than or equal to x
floor(x) - the greatest integer less than or equal to x
round(x) - x rounded to the nearest integer
random() - a random number between zero and one

These are the base constants and functions that are available in a direct call through the JavaScript interface. Other math functions may be derived by utilizing these methods and properties in the equation.
